④ Overcome
Step 1 — Understand the Objection
"What about that plan didn't you like?"
Engage Actively Discover Needs
Only needed when the objection is nonspecific ("I'm not interested"). A specific objection ("too expensive") goes straight to Step 2. Skipping Step 1 on a vague objection is the most common Overcome scoring gap.
Step 2 — Empathize and Overcome
"I completely understand that, however, with our services..."
Engage Actively Own It
Empathy must come first — agent names what the customer said before pivoting. "I understand" followed immediately by a pitch is performative, not genuine.
Step 3 — Restate Key Tie-Backs
"I heard you say [X], so I wanted to make sure..."
Discover Needs Ambassador
Re-anchors the customer to what they said in Explore. The tie-back must match something the customer actually told the agent — not a generic benefit.
Step 4 — Add Additional Value
"One other thing about that product you might like is..."
Ambassador Own It
Introduces a benefit not yet mentioned — gives the customer a new reason to reconsider. Should feel additive, not desperate.
⑤ Review
Summarize all services on the order, promotional pricing & duration, and roll-to rate.
