The Power of the First 30 Seconds

Day 1Positive Introduction
Morning Huddle 30 min High Energy

Run of Show

  1. Open with energy 2 min - Stand up, music playing as agents walk in. Set the tone that this week is different.
  2. Frame the week 3 min - "This week we're doing a 5-day bootcamp. Every day, one topic. Morning huddle, midday check-ins, afternoon debrief. By Friday, you'll own the Compass Navigator call flow."
  3. Today's topic: The Opening 8 min - Walk through why the first 30 seconds matter. Outbound is an interruption. The customer didn't ask for this call. You have seconds to earn their attention.
    • Play a bad example (robotic, flat, rushed) vs. a good example (warm, natural, personal connection)
    • Ask the room: "Which agent would you stay on the line for?"
  4. The Compass Navigator opening script 5 min - Walk through the scripted opening. Emphasize the natural delivery, not memorized recitation. Point out the window for personal connection between getting the name and stating the reason for the call.
    "Hello! My name is [First Last], and I'm calling from Xfinity. May I speak with [Customer Name]?"
    [Confirm identity, ask how their day is going]
    "Before we continue, I need to inform you that the call is being recorded and monitored..."
  5. Existing customer bonus 3 min - If they already have Xfinity, using their name early shows you prepped. "I see you've been with us for 3 years" is a power move. It costs nothing and changes the call.
  6. Recording disclosure 3 min - Must happen before discovery. Practice saying it naturally at a conversational pace. If it sounds rushed, slow it down.
  7. Preview the activity 1 min - "This afternoon, you're going to pair up and practice your opening. Right now, think about what you want yours to sound like."
Facilitator Tip: Don't lecture. Ask questions. "Who here has had a customer hang up in the first 10 seconds? What do you think happened?" Get them talking early so the huddle feels like a conversation, not a presentation.
Day 1 Activity: The 30-Second Challenge Built into afternoon

Setup

  • Pair agents up. One plays the customer, one plays the agent.
  • Customer answers the phone with a neutral "Hello?"
  • Agent delivers their full opening: greeting, name, company, ask for the right person, personal connection, recording disclosure.
  • Time limit: 30 seconds. Use a stopwatch.

Rules

  • The opening must include a genuine personal connection moment (not just "How are you?")
  • Recording disclosure must be delivered before the 30-second mark
  • After each round, the "customer" gives one piece of feedback: what made them want to stay on the line, or what made them want to hang up

Debrief Questions

  • "What felt different about doing it live vs. reading it?"
  • "What did your partner do that made you actually want to listen?"
Midday Floor Walk Throughout the day

What to Listen For

  • Is the greeting warm or robotic?
  • Is the recording disclosure delivered before discovery questions?
  • Are agents using the customer's name on existing customer calls?
  • Is there a pause for personal connection or does it jump straight to pitch?

Quick Coaching Interventions

  • Agent sounds flat? Have them stand up for their next call.
  • Disclosure sounds rushed? Practice at half speed, then bring it up naturally.
  • No personal connection? Challenge them: "Ask one non-scripted question in your next opening."
Afternoon Focus 15 min Reflective
  1. Run the 30-Second Challenge activity 8 min
  2. Round-table debrief 5 min - "What's one thing you changed about your opening today? What happened?"
  3. Set the challenge for tomorrow 2 min - "Record yourself doing your opening before your first call tomorrow morning. Listen back. Would you stay on the line?"
Commitments You're Looking For
  1. Smile before you dial. Tone changes when you smile. The customer hears it.
  2. Use their name within the first 15 seconds on existing customer calls. It signals pre-call preparation.
  3. Pause after "How are you?" Actually listen. Respond to what they say before moving on.
  4. Deliver the recording disclosure naturally. Practice until it sounds conversational, not read.
Success Metric: By end of day, every agent should be able to deliver a warm, natural opening with recording disclosure in under 30 seconds without sounding scripted.

Discovering with Intent

Day 2Qualitative vs. Quantitative
Morning Huddle 30 min High Energy

Run of Show

  1. Quick Day 1 callback 2 min - "Who recorded themselves this morning? How did it sound?" Quick show of hands or call on 1-2 people.
  2. Frame today's topic 3 min - "Yesterday was about getting them to stay. Today is about learning enough to make them want to buy." Discovery is the AGENT asking questions, not the customer asking you.
  3. The 4 Pillars 8 min - Walk through Users, Usage, Devices, Cost with examples for each:
    Users: "Who all is in the household?"
    Usage: "Walk me through a typical evening online."
    Devices: "How many devices are connected at once?"
    Cost: "What are you paying for Internet and phone combined?"
  4. Qualitative vs. Quantitative demo 8 min - This is the core lesson. Do a live comparison:
    • Bad: "Do you have WiFi?" / "Do you stream?" / "Do you have a phone?" = three questions, zero insight
    • Good: "Tell me about your household. Who's home during the day and what are they usually doing online?" = one question, three pillars uncovered
    Ask the room: "Which agent knows more about the customer after their questions?"
  5. Key rule 2 min - One great open-ended question beats five closed ones. Quality over quantity. Discovery should feel like a conversation, not an interrogation.
  6. Tie-backs preview 3 min - "Everything the customer tells you in discovery becomes ammunition for your pitch tomorrow. Write it down. When they say 'my kids stream all day,' that's a tie-back you'll use in the Consult."
  7. Preview the activity 2 min
Facilitator Tip: Have two agents role-play discovery live in front of the group. One asks only closed questions. The other asks open-ended. After both, ask the group which agent learned more. The contrast sells the lesson better than any slide.
Day 2 Activity: Discovery Bingo Built into afternoon

Setup

  • Give each agent a simple 2x2 grid with the 4 pillars: Users, Usage, Devices, Cost
  • Pair up. One is the customer (use a persona card: e.g., "You're a single mom with 2 kids, one is a gamer, you're paying $120/mo for AT&T")
  • The agent has 3 minutes to fill all 4 boxes through open-ended questions only
  • If the agent asks a closed question (yes/no), the customer calls it out and the agent must rephrase

Debrief

  • "How many pillars did you uncover? What was hardest to get to?"
  • "What question worked best? Share it with the group."
Midday Floor Walk Throughout the day
  • Are agents asking open-ended or closed questions?
  • Are all 4 pillars being covered before the pitch?
  • Are agents noting tie-backs for use in the Consult?
  • Does discovery sound like a conversation or an intake form?

Quick Coaching

  • Agent jumps to pitch after one question: "What else could you have asked before presenting?"
  • Discovery sounds like a survey: "React to what they say. If they mention kids, ask about the kids."
Afternoon Focus 15 min Reflective
  1. Run Discovery Bingo 8 min
  2. Share-out 5 min - "What's the most interesting thing you learned about a customer today through discovery?"
  3. Challenge for tomorrow 2 min - "Try to get a customer to tell you a story about their household. Not answers. A story."
Commitments You're Looking For
  1. Ask at least one open-ended question per pillar. If you only get to two pillars, make them Users and Usage.
  2. Listen for tie-backs. When the customer says something, write it down. You'll use it in the pitch.
  3. Don't interrogate. Discovery should feel like a conversation. React to what they say.
  4. Never skip discovery. A pitch without discovery is a feature list. A pitch with discovery is a recommendation.
Success Metric: By end of day, agents should be asking predominantly open-ended questions and covering at least 3 of 4 pillars before pitching.

Linking & Being the Ambassador

Day 3From Discovery to Recommendation
Morning Huddle 30 min High Energy

Run of Show

  1. Day 2 callback 2 min - "Did anyone get a customer to tell them a story yesterday? What did you learn?"
  2. Frame today 3 min - "Yesterday you learned how to uncover needs. Today you learn how to USE what you uncovered. This is where discovery becomes a sale."
  3. The logic bridge 8 min - Every recommendation needs a "because." Walk through the Consult formula:
    "Based on what you told me, I think I have the perfect plan..."
    "I heard you say [X], so I included [product] because [benefit]."

    Do a live demo: take a fake customer profile and build the pitch using tie-backs in real time. Show the difference between a feature list and a tailored recommendation.

  4. Feature list vs. recommendation 5 min - Show both side by side:
    • Bad: "Our 1 Gig plan comes with unlimited data, a gateway, and Advanced Security. $60 a month."
    • Good: "You mentioned your husband works from home and your kids stream constantly, so I'm recommending the 1 Gig plan because your whole household can be online at once without anyone lagging. And since you said you're paying $80 now, this actually brings you down to $60."
  5. Product confidence 5 min - You can't pitch what you don't know. Quick product knowledge check:
    • What's included in the WiFi deal at each tier?
    • What's the XM line promo worth? ($480 over 12 months)
    • When do you mention broadband labels?
  6. Broadband labels compliance 3 min - Required on every Internet or Mobile offer. Weave it into the pitch naturally: "You can also see this information at Xfinity.com/labels."
  7. Preview activity 2 min
Day 3 Activity: 60-Second Pitch Build Built into afternoon

Setup

  • Prepare 4-5 customer persona cards with discovery details already filled in (e.g., "Family of 4, heavy gamers, currently on AT&T at $90/mo, 3 phones and a tablet")
  • Each agent draws a card and has 60 seconds to build and deliver a pitch using tie-backs
  • The pitch MUST include: "Based on what you told me..." and at least one "I heard you say..."
  • Must reference broadband labels

Judging Criteria (group votes)

  • Did the pitch connect features to the customer's specific life?
  • Would you buy based on that pitch?
  • Was broadband labels included naturally?
Midday Floor Walk Throughout the day
  • Are agents using tie-back language? ("You mentioned...", "I heard you say...")
  • Is the pitch connecting features to discovered needs or listing features generically?
  • Are broadband labels being referenced during the pitch?
  • Is the right product being pitched for the household?
Afternoon Focus 15 min Competitive
  1. Run the 60-Second Pitch Build 10 min
  2. Group vote on best pitch 3 min
  3. Challenge for tomorrow 2 min - "Start every pitch tomorrow with 'Based on what you told me...' See how it changes the customer's response."
Commitments You're Looking For
  1. Never pitch without a "because." If you can't finish "I'm recommending this because you said...", you didn't discover enough.
  2. Use the customer's exact words. "You mentioned..." and "I heard you say..." are the most powerful phrases in the Consult.
  3. Lead with the benefit, not the feature. "Your kids can stream without buffering" beats "It comes with unlimited data."
  4. Reference broadband labels naturally. Practice weaving it in so it doesn't sound like compliance.
Success Metric: By end of day, agents should be using at least one tie-back per pitch and referencing broadband labels on every Internet/Mobile offer.

Objections & Overcoming Them

Day 4The 4-Step Framework
Morning Huddle 30 min High Energy

Run of Show

  1. Day 3 callback 2 min - "Who started with 'Based on what you told me' yesterday? How did the customer react?"
  2. Frame today 3 min - "An objection is NOT a rejection. It's the customer telling you they need more information. The agents who close the most aren't the ones who never hear 'no.' They're the ones who know what to do when they hear it."
  3. The 4-Step Framework 12 min - This is the core of today. Walk through each step with a live example:
    1. Understand - Don't assume. Probe. "What specifically concerns you about that?"
    2. Empathize & Overcome - "I completely understand that, however..."
    3. Restate Tie-Backs - "I heard you say [X], so I wanted to make sure..."
    4. Add Value - Introduce one new benefit they haven't heard

    Do a live demo: facilitator plays the customer, an agent walks through all 4 steps. Then debrief with the group.

  4. Common objections walkthrough 6 min
    "I'll think about it" - "Absolutely. What part are you thinking over?"

    "Too expensive" - Reframe: "That's $2/day for your whole household."
    "Under contract" - "When does it end?" Then explore timing.

    "Not interested" - "Is it the product itself, or is this just not a good time?"
  5. Critical rule 2 min - Never speak negatively about competitors. That's a red flag (auto-fail). "Our service is 99.9% reliable" is fine. "AT&T is terrible" is not.
  6. The re-close 3 min - After overcoming, you MUST ask again. Don't leave the sale hanging. Close with the same confidence as the first time.
Facilitator Tip: The biggest mistake agents make is going straight to rebuttal without understanding the real objection. Drill Step 1 (Understand) harder than the others. If they get the real reason, the rest flows naturally.
Day 4 Activity: Objection Gauntlet Built into afternoon

Setup

  • Prepare objection cards: "I need to think about it", "Too expensive", "I'm under contract", "Not interested", "I need to talk to my spouse", "I already have a provider"
  • Agent sits in the hot seat. Facilitator (or another agent) draws a card and delivers the objection.
  • Agent must walk through ALL 4 steps in order: Understand, Empathize, Restate, Add Value
  • Then re-close. The "customer" decides if they'd stay on the line.
  • Rotate through 3-4 agents. Group gives feedback after each.

What to Watch For

  • Did they probe before rebutting? (Step 1 is the one most agents skip)
  • Did empathy sound genuine or scripted?
  • Did they use a tie-back from discovery? (They can make one up for this exercise)
  • Did they re-close?
Midday Floor Walk Throughout the day
  • When an objection comes up, does the agent probe or immediately rebut?
  • After overcoming, does the agent re-close or just move on?
  • Does "I understand" sound genuine or reflexive?
  • Is the agent using tie-backs from discovery in their rebuttal?
Afternoon Focus 15 min Competitive
  1. Run the Objection Gauntlet 10 min
  2. Group discussion 3 min - "What's the hardest objection you heard today? How did you handle it?"
  3. Challenge for tomorrow 2 min - "When you hear 'I'll think about it' tomorrow, your first response must be a question, not a statement."
Commitments You're Looking For
  1. Always probe before rebutting. "What specifically concerns you?" changes the conversation.
  2. Lead with empathy. "I completely understand" - and mean it.
  3. Use their own words in the rebuttal. Tie-backs from discovery are your strongest tool.
  4. Always re-close after overcoming. Don't leave the sale hanging. Ask again with confidence.
Success Metric: By end of day, agents should be probing before rebutting on every objection and re-closing after every overcome attempt.

Putting It All Together

Day 5Full Call Flow Mastery
Morning Huddle 30 min High Energy

Run of Show

  1. Week recap 5 min - Quick-fire review. Point at the board/screen:
    • Day 1: First 30 seconds. Smile, name, connection, disclosure.
    • Day 2: Discovery. Open-ended. 4 pillars. Conversation, not interrogation.
    • Day 3: Linking. Tie-backs. "Because." Lead with benefits.
    • Day 4: Objections. Understand first. Empathize. Restate. Add value. Re-close.
    "Today we connect all four into one complete call."
  2. The Compass Navigator flow 5 min - Walk through all 5 phases: Connect, Explore, Consult, Overcome, Review. Show how each day's topic maps to a phase. The customer shouldn't feel the transitions.
  3. The 6 Catalyst Behaviors self-check 8 min - Go through each one. For each, ask the room: "On a scale of 1-5, where are you right now on this behavior?"
    • Engage Actively - Am I treating this as a conversation or a transaction?
    • Own It - Am I demonstrating expertise and keeping the customer informed?
    • Make It Effortless - Am I delivering information in digestible pieces?
    • Discover Needs - Am I asking questions that reveal something new?
    • Be an Ambassador - Am I connecting products to this customer's life?
    • Show Appreciation - Does the customer feel valued, not processed?
  4. Going forward message 5 min - This isn't the end of training. It's the beginning of intentional improvement. Pick one behavior each day. Master it. Move to the next. The QA scorecard is a mirror, not a judgment.
  5. Preview the final activity 2 min
Day 5 Activity: Full Call Simulation Built into afternoon

Setup

  • Prepare 3-4 detailed customer personas (name, current provider, household, budget, likely objection)
  • One agent volunteers for the hot seat. Facilitator plays the customer using the persona.
  • Agent must run the entire call flow: Opening, Discovery, Pitch with tie-backs, Handle the objection, Re-close, Summarize
  • The rest of the group scores on paper: Did they hit each phase? What was strongest? What was the gap?
  • Run 2-3 agents if time allows

Debrief

  • Group gives feedback: strongest phase and biggest gap
  • Facilitator highlights one thing each agent did well that they should keep doing
Midday Floor Walk Throughout the day
  • Listen to complete calls today, not just segments
  • Does the call flow through all 5 Compass Navigator phases?
  • Identify each agent's strongest phase and biggest gap
  • Note 1-2 agents to recognize in the afternoon wrap-up
Afternoon Focus 15 min Celebratory
  1. Run Full Call Simulation 8 min
  2. Recognition 3 min - Call out agents who showed the most improvement or effort this week. Be specific about what you observed.
  3. Personal commitment 4 min - Each agent writes down one specific behavior they will sustain going forward. Post it at their desk. This is their personal contract with themselves.
Bootcamp Commitments - Full Week

Day 1: Opening

  1. Smile before you dial
  2. Use their name in the first 15 seconds
  3. Pause after "How are you?" and listen
  4. Recording disclosure delivered naturally

Day 2: Discovery

  1. Open-ended questions covering 4 pillars
  2. Listen for and record tie-backs
  3. Conversation, not interrogation
  4. Never skip discovery

Day 3: Linking

  1. Every pitch has a "because"
  2. Use the customer's exact words
  3. Benefits over features
  4. Broadband labels woven in naturally

Day 4: Objections

  1. Probe before rebutting
  2. Lead with genuine empathy
  3. Tie-backs in the rebuttal
  4. Always re-close after overcoming
Post-Bootcamp: Use the Catalyst QA Scorecard to track these behaviors weekly. Share results in team huddles. Celebrate ED scores. Coach on DND patterns. The bootcamp plants the seed. Consistent reinforcement grows it.
